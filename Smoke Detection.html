IMPACT OF GAS EMISSION

Air Pollution and Health Hazards:

Vehicle emissions release harmful pollutants like CO₂, CO, NOx, and particulate matter into the atmosphere. These pollutants contribute to respiratory diseases, heart conditions, and premature deaths. Prolonged exposure to such emissions increases the risk of chronic health issues in urban populations. .

Climate Change and Global Warming:

Excessive emissions of greenhouse gases from vehicles significantly contribute to climate change. The accumulation of CO₂ in the atmosphere traps heat, leading to global temperature rise. This results in severe environmental consequences like melting ice caps, rising sea levels, and extreme weather patterns.

Environmental Degradation:

Vehicle emissions contribute to acid rain, which damages ecosystems, buildings, and agriculture. The deposition of pollutants on soil and water bodies affects biodiversity and the quality of natural resources. Over time, this leads to the degradation of habitats, affecting wildlife and plant life.

Economic Impact:

The health problems caused by vehicle emissions result in increased healthcare costs for individuals and governments. Additionally, pollution control measures and infrastructure improvements to combat emissions impose financial burdens. The reduced air quality also affects workforce productivity and tourism in polluted regions.
